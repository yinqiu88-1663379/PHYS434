In ATLAS , Beams of particles travelling at energies up to seven trillion electron-volts, or speeds up to 99.999999% that of light, from the LHC collide at the centre of the ATLAS detector producing collision debris in the form of new particles which fly out in all directions.
ATLAS detector is a many-layered instrument designed to detect some of the tiniest yet most energetic particles ever created on earth.

It consists of six different detecting subsystems wrapped concentrically in layers around the collision point to record the trajectory, momentum, and energy of particles, allowing them to be individually identified and measured. A huge magnet system bends the paths of the charged particles so that their momenta can be measured as precisely as possible.

According to Standard Model, the Higgs mechanism, which includes the Higgs boson, gives mass to elementary particles, leading to differences between the weak force and electromagnetism by giving the W and Z bosons mass while leaving the photon massless. Higgs boson would decay into two photons and its decay to four leptons.

In order to declare the existence of Higgs boson, we need to make the experience in ATLAS The data we get is the jet which is the effect of collusion. After two beam particle collusion together, the particles might broken into lots of Higgs, called jets. Those jets will be recorded by the side surface of ATLAS.

Several information of jets would be recorded. There are two parts of data. One is from QCD the other is from Higgs which is the signal we are looking for. Since they have differents characteristics, we can distingushing them and declair a signal when we get data from background.

ee2/ee3¶

ECF ratio, energy-correlation functions Energy Correlation Funcitons ratio is in the form of ee2 = sum(pt_i pt_j (delta R_ij)^b) where i<j belongs to jet. And the beta is the weight of the angular separation of the jet constituents. The delta R_ij is the distance between two jet constituents. ee3 = sum(pt_i pt_j pt_k) (delta R_ij delta R _jk * delta R_ik)^b ) d2 is 3-to-2 Point ECF ratio which is in the form of ee3/(ee2)^3

It's really convenient to use the energy correlation function because it does not require a choice of reference axes. At this accuracy, angularities and ECFs have the same structure, provided one uses a recoil-insensitive jet axis definition for angularities with 1

‘t1’/‘t2’/'t3'/‘t21'/‘t32'¶

The Higgs boson will decay to bottom quark pair and each of quark form a jet. If the higges boson has large momentumn and two of them has same direction, several quark will combine to one jet. The t1/t2/t3 represents different kinds of N-subjetiness and helps us to indentify what kinds to subjets it is.
